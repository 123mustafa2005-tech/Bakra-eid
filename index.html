Bakra Eid, also known as Eid-ul-Adha, is one of the most important Islamic festivals celebrated by Muslims around the world. It honors the sacrifice and obedience of Prophet Ibrahim (A.S), who was willing to sacrifice his son for the sake of Allah. On this special occasion, Muslims offer prayers, sacrifice animals such as goats, cows, or camels, and distribute the meat among family, friends, and the needy. Bakra Eid teaches lessons of faith, sacrifice, kindness, and sharing with others. Families gather together, wear new clothes, enjoy delicious food, and celebrate the day with happiness and gratitude.
